## Abstract

### SPAC Dynamical Model Stimation

This project analyzes a dynamical system based on the Soil–Plant–Atmosphere Continuum (SPAC) concept, which describes the physical interactions between soil, plant and atmosphere. The main objective is to estimate the internal states of a mathematical model representing biomass growth, plant internal water content and soil water availability.

The model consists of a nonlinear system of three differential equations representing mass and energy balances within the SPAC framework. Observability analysis and equilibrium points are determined, and the system is linearized around these operating points in order to obtain the state-space representation required for the design of state estimators.

The mathematical formulation used in this work corresponds to the model proposed by Díaz-González et al. (2022) and López-Jiménez et al. (2022), which describes the coupled dynamics between biomass production, plant hydration processes and soil moisture availability. This framework enables the analysis and implementation of estimation strategies for ecological and agricultural dynamical systems.
